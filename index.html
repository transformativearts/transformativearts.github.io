The Transformation

'Transformative Arts' refers to the creative process by which Practitioners engage Participants in artistic activities with the purpose of promoting positive change in the lives of all who participate, as well those who witness what is created.

The Arts

The activities facilitated by Transformative Arts Practitioners encompass both expressive and contemplative practices, including puppetry and poetry, dance and drama, music and story-telling, painting and sculpting, prayer and meditation, chant and mantra.

The Practitioners

Practitioners of Transformative Arts are artists of one kind or another. They may also be educators or therapists, clinicians or advocates, but not necessarily. It is the primacy of artistic practice and creative process that distinguishes Transformative Arts from other disciplines.

For Individuals

A Practitioner can facilitate a Transformative Arts process for a single participant, by sitting at the side of a traumatized child and enabling them to draw or paint what cannot be spoken, or helping someone in their final days compile a collage of mementos that will remain long after their passing.

For Groups

A Practitioner can facilitate a Transformative Arts process for a group of people, by engaging those of conflicting beliefs in producing a collaborative opera, or by helping wounded victims of war paint a mural.

Relief from Hurt

Transformative Arts can help Participants grieve their losses, lament their mistakes, voice their remorse, and acknowledge their sorrows, and in so doing find relief from the burden of untold hurt.

Giving Voice

Transformative Arts can help oppressed and disenfranchised Participants find a voice with which to claim rightful justice, and help those who have been displaced rediscover a sense of belonging.

Finding a Language

Transformative Arts can help Participants find a language of expression most suited to their personality, encompassing sound and silence, stillness and movement, mask and costume, shape and color.

Releasing Imagination

Transformative Arts can release participants from the constraints of explanation and logic, opening up the domains of imagery and metaphor, possibility and paradox, through which the deepest values and highest aspirations of humanity are expressed.

Protest and Peace

Transformative Arts have played a significant role in providing a peaceful platform of protest and route to reconciliation in diverse conflict-smitten locations, including South Africa, El Salvador, Northern Ireland, Cambodia, and Bosnia.

Healing Distress

Transformative Arts have played a principal role in alleviating the distress of those facing major challenges, including anxiety and depression, physical disability and injury, terminal illness and incurable disease.

Creativity

Transformative Arts can enable anyone, anywhere, at any time to create, and thereby offers a positive path through even the most negative circumstances.

A Brief Background

Transformative Arts

Prehistoric - Ancient - Modern

Transformative Arts have a prehistoric, ancient, and modern history.

Prehistorically, they grew from the tendency of Homo sapiens to seek meaning in seemingly inexplicable experiences through myth-making, bringing supranatural characters and archetypal narratives to life through painting, dancing, music, and drama.

These arts are integral to all known communities, where they developed not as forms of entertainment made by one group of active artists for the passive consumption of others, but as the means by which everyone participated in an active process of creative expression.

In modern history, Transformative Arts found an underlying consolidated paradigm and purpose during the mid-twentieth century, through the work of creative educators, agitators, activists, and artists whose work dissembled the boundaries between professional and non-professional artistry, recruiting individuals and communities to the process of making music, dance, theatre, paintings, and sculpture.

Today, Transformative Arts Practitioners work in a variety of contexts, including community centers and hospitals, prisons and schools, enabling others to discover their own potential as artists, whilst finding the potential of the arts to facilitate positive personal and social transformation.

The Transformative Arts Society

ISTA provides a representative voice for Practitioners of Transformative Arts, whilst compiling a database of resources to support training and practice.
